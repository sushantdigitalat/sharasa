What is the 64 Kalas Competition?

 
The 64 Kalas are a diverse collection of traditional Indian art forms, skills, and disciplines, each representing a unique facet of human creativity and expression. Rooted in ancient Indian wisdom, these Kalas were designed to nurture a person’s holistic development—encompassing physical, intellectual, emotional, and spiritual growth. The concept of the 64 Kalas was first introduced in classical Indian literature, with references in texts such as the Kamasutra, the Natyashastra, and various Puranas, symbolizing a well-rounded and refined education system that went far beyond mere academics.

By participating in this competition, your child can:

5.Develop new skills through structured, age-appropriate assignments designed to enhance motor skills, cognitive abilities, and artistic expression.

7.Connect with Indian heritage by learning and practicing art forms that have been passed down through generations.

4.Foster a sense of discipline and focus by working on tasks that require patience, attention to detail, and practice.

6.Gain exposure to diverse forms of art including visual arts (painting, drawing, sculpture), performing arts (music, dance, drama), and literary arts (poetry, storytelling).

Why to join 64 Kalas? and Outcomes

The essence of the 64 Kalas is to provide a well-rounded foundation for children, encouraging them to develop in all aspects of life beyond just academics. This approach:
  • Encourages Creativity: Nurtures imaginative thinking and problem-solving skills.
  • Builds Cultural Awareness: Introduces children to India’s rich artistic and cultural heritage.
  • Develops Motor Skills: Many Kalas, such as painting, crafting, and dancing, help children improve their fine and gross motor skills.
  • Improves Cognitive Development: The Kalas stimulate various areas of the brain, fostering intellectual curiosity.
  • Promotes Emotional Well-being: Art forms like music and storytelling help children express their emotions, improving emotional regulation and communication.
  • Boosts Confidence: By mastering new skills and performing in front of others, children gain self-assurance and a sense of accomplishment.
